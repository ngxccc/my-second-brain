# Academic Critique: Electricity Grids Creak as AI Demands Soar

## Introduction

AI (Artificial Intelligence) is growing very fast. Because of this, computer data centers need a lot of electricity. In the article "Electricity grids creak as AI demands soar", Chris Baraniuk writes that data centers will need double the electricity by 2026. This document looks at four ideas from the article and explains why these ideas might have problems.

---

## Critique and Counter-Argument Table

| Key Arguments / Claims in the Article                                                                                                                                                                                                                       | Academic Critique & Counter-Arguments                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1. Nvidia Blackwell Chips Will Save Energy**<br>The article says that new Nvidia Grace Blackwell chips use less electricity to do the same work (4 megawatts instead of 15 megawatts). Some people say this will save a lot of energy.                    | **1. More AI Use and High Energy Demand**<br>When technology gets better and cheaper, people use it much more. This is called Jevons' Paradox. Because of this, we will use more electricity in total. Also, training one big AI still uses a huge amount of electricity (8.6 GWh), which is as much as a whole city uses in a week. |
| **2. AI Does Not Care About Latency, So Data Centers Can Go to Remote Places**<br>The article says that AI does not need to be fast (low latency). So, data centers can go to remote places near power plants or wind energy (like Iowa).                   | **2. Power Line Problems and Local Needs**<br>Sending electricity over long distances is hard and can break the power lines. Also, data centers still need water to cool down the computers. They also compete with local people who need electricity for their homes.                                                               |
| **3. Good Energy Scores and Warming Swimming Pools Help the Environment**<br>The article says data centers are very efficient. They have a good energy score (PUE of 1.1). In Europe, they use the hot air from computers to warm up public swimming pools. | **3. Big Energy Use and Small Solutions**<br>A good score does not mean they use less energy in total. Giant data centers still use too much electricity. Also, heating swimming pools is a very small project. It cannot solve the huge electricity problem of big data centers.                                                    |
| **4. High Costs Will Stop People from Using Too Much AI**<br>The article suggests that high prices will limit AI. If old computers are cheaper, people will choose them instead of expensive AI.                                                            | **4. The AI Race Will Drive Big Energy Consumption**<br>Tech companies are in a big race to win the AI market. They want to be the best and fastest. They will spend a lot of money and use a lot of energy to train AI, even if they lose money. So, market costs will not stop them.                                               |
